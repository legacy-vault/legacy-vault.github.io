Vocabulary.

LIST OF COMPUTER RELATED TERMS

AMD.
Advanced Micro Devices. An American Company working in Computer Industry. Famous for "AMD64" Instructions Set and AMD Athlon CPU.

Apple Inc.
An American Company working in Computer Industry. Was incorporated as "Apple Computer, Inc." on January 3, 1977.

B.
Byte. Written as a capital Letter "B".

b.
Bit. Written as a small Letter "b".

Bit.
Binary Digit. A basic Unit of Information in binary Computers.

Binary.
That which uses only two Values. Mostly "True or False", "1 or 0", 1000101010. Most modern Computers are binary.

Byte.
In most Cases is equal to 8 Bits.

CD.
Compact Disk. A digital optical Disk Data Storage Format. Standard Diameter of 120 mm (4,7 in) and Capacity from 650 to 700 MiB.

CD-ROM.
Compact Disk Read-Only Memory. Type of CD Disk which is Read-only (cannot be re-written or erased).

CD-ROM Drive.
A Device working with Disks of CD-ROM Type.

Computer.
A general-purpose Device that can be programmed to carry out a Set of arithmetic or logical Operations automatically.

Corporation.
A company or group of people authorized to act as a single Entity and recognized as such in Law.

CPU.
Central Processing Unit, Central Processor of a Computer.

Creative Technology Limited.
A Company producing Computer audio Devices, headquartered in Singapore.

d.
Day. Time/Event Measuring Unit. One (1) full Turn of a Planet around its own Axis. On Earth Planet, 1 d = ~24 h.

DOS.
Disk Operating System. Mostly associated with MSDOS.

DVD.
Digital versatile Disk, or Digital Video Disk. A digital optical Disk Data Storage Format. Standard Diameter of 120 mm (4,7 in) and Capacity of 4,37 GiB.

DVD-ROM.
DVD Read-Only Memory. Type of DVD Disk which is Read-only (cannot be re-written or erased).

DVD-ROM Drive.
A Device working with Disks of DVD-ROM Type.

Floppy Disk.
A Floppy Disk, a Diskette, or just a Floppy, is a Type of Disk Storage composed of a Disk of thin and flexible magnetic Storage Medium, sealed in a rectangular plastic Carrier lined with fabric that removes Dust Particles. Floppy Disks are read and written by a Floppy Disk Drive (FDD). Floppy Disks, initially as 8-Inch (8.0", 200 mm) Media and later in 5¼-Inch (5.25", 133 mm) and 3½-Inch (3.5", 90 mm) Sizes, were a ubiquitous Form of Data Storage and Exchange from the Middle of 1970s well into the 2000s. First 8.0" Diskettes were commercially available in Year 1971. Diskettes of the most popular 3.5" HD Format used 1.2 µm (1200 nm) cobalt-doped iron oxide.

By the Year 2010, Computer Motherboards are rarely manufactured with Floppy Drive Support; 3½-inch (3.5") Floppy Disks can be used with an external USB Floppy Disk Drive (USB FDD), but USB Drives for 5¼-Inch (5.25"), 8-Inch (8.0"), and non-standard Diskettes are rare to non-existent. These Formats are usually handled by older Equipment. While Floppy Disk Drives still have some limited Uses, especially with legacy industrial Computer Equipment, they have been superseded by Data Storage Methods with much greater Capacity, such as USB Flash Sticks, Flash Storage Cards, portable external Hard Disk Drives (HDD), Optical Discs (OD), and Computer Networks. One of the latest Models of the Motherboards that have FDD Connector on the Board is an ASRock 980DE3/U3S3 (it supports Socket AM3+ AMD's CPUs with TDP up to 140W, 4 Modules of DDRIII DIMM SDRAM, 6x SATA2 and 2x SATA3, 4x PCI Express 2.0, 3x USB2.0 and 1x USB3.0, 2x PS/2, 1x COM, 1x ATA133 IDE, 1x Floppy and good old 16 MB AMI Legal BIOS).

The latest and most popular Format of Diskettes was introduced in the Year 1987 and it was a 3½-Inch (3.5") HD Format with the Formatted Storage Capacity of 1440 kB (2880 Sectors of 512 Bytes each) and 1.44 MB as marked Capacity. "HD" in the Name means High Density. In the Year 1999 Sony Corporation introduced the 3½-Inch (3.5") HiFD Format, having the Formatted Storage Capacity of 150/200 MB. This Format was backward compatible with existing 3.5" Floppies (old Devices could use it as a 1.44 MB Disk). By the strange historical Reasons, all the Improvements of the Floppy-sized Formats have not become popular in the World.

GB.
Gigabyte. Decimal Prefix. (10 x 10 = 100). 1 GB = 1'000 MB = 1'000'000 KB = 1'000'000'000 B.

GiB.
Gibibyte. Binary Prefix. (2 x 2 = 4). 1 GiB = 1'024 MiB = 1'048'576 KiB = 1'073'741'824 B.

Hardware.
Literally, a Collection of physical Elements that constitutes something. Computer Hardware is the physical Parts or Components of a Computer.

h.
Hour. Time Measuring Unit. 1 h = 60 min.

HDD.
Hard Disk Drive. A Storage Device using magnetic Disks.

Hz.
Hertz. Frequency Measuring Unit. 1 Hz = 1 Cycle per Second.

IBM.
International Business Machines. An American Company working in the Sphere of electronic Computer Devices Industry.

IBM PC.
A first massively produced Computer by IBM that used an i8088 CPU. CPU i8088 was based on i8086 CPU that used "x86" Instructions Set.

in.
Inch. Distance Measuring Unit. 1 in = 2,54 mm.

Inc.
Means -- Incorporated. Incorporation is the forming of a new Corporation.

Intel.
Intel Corporation, an American Company producing electronic Devices for Computers. Best known for its "Intel x86" Instructions Set and Intel Pentium CPU.

ISO 8601.
Time Format by ISO. Mostly known as "YYYY-MM-DD" Format, where YYYY is Year, MM is Month, DD is Day.

ISO 9660.
ISO 9660, also referred to as CDFS (Compact Disc File System) in Microsoft Windows, is a File System Standard published by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) for optical Disc Media. It aims at supporting different Computer Operating Systems such as Windows, classic Mac OS, and Unix-like Systems, so that Data may be exchanged. ISO 9660 traces its Roots to the High Sierra Format File System, which was adopted (with Changes) in December 1986 as an international Standard by Ecma International as ECMA-119 and submitted for fast tracking to the ISO, where it was eventually accepted as ISO 9660:1988. In Year 2013, ISO published Amendment 1 to the ISO 9660 Standard, introducing new Data Structures and relaxed File Name Rules intended to "bring Harmonization between ISO 9660 and widely used Joliet Specification".

KB.
Kilobyte. Decimal Prefix. (10 x 10 = 100). 1 KB = 1'000 B.

KiB.
Kibibyte. Binary Prefix. (2 x 2 = 4). 1 KiB = 1'024 B.

Mac OS.
A Series of closed-sourced Operating Systems developed by Apple Inc. for their Macintosh Line of Computer Systems.

MB.
Megabyte. Decimal Prefix. (10 x 10 = 100). 1 MB = 1'000 KB. 1 KB = 1'000 B. Thus, 1 MB = 1'000'000 B.

MHz.
Megahertz. Frequency Measuring Unit. 1 MHz = 1'000'000 Hz. Mostly used as a Frequency of CPU's internal Clock.

MiB.
Mebibyte. Binary Prefix. (2 x 2 = 4). 1 MiB = 1'024 KiB. 1 KiB = 1'024 B. Thus, 1 MiB = 1'048'576 B.

m.
Meter. Distance Measuring Unit.

micro
μ, (from Ancient Greek μικρός ‎(mikrós, "small"), one Millionth Part of Something. 1m = 1'000'000 μm.

min.
Minute. Time Measuring Unit. 1 min = 60 sec.

mm.
Millimeter. 1'000 mm = 1 m.

ms.
Millisecond. Time Measuring Unit. 1'000 ms = 1 sec.

MS.
Microsoft Corporation. An American Company best known for its closed-sourced Programs, most of which are Operating Systems and Office Software.

MSDOS.
Disk Operating System (DOS), an OS produced by MS.

Optical Disk.
A flat, usually circular Disk which encodes binary Data (bits) in the Form of Pits and Lands on a special Material (often Aluminium) on one of its flat Surfaces.

OS.
O.S., Operating System. Computer's main Program that runs and directs other Programs.

PC.
Personal Computer. At present Time, means not literally a personal Piece of Hardware, but instead, an IBM PC.

Pentium.
A Series of CPUs released by Intel in 1993-03-22.

RAM.
Random Access Memory. Type of Memory used in modern Computers.

sec.
Second. Time Measuring Unit. Under the "International System of Units", since Year 1967, the Second has been defined as the Duration of 9'192'631'770 Periods of the Radiation corresponding to the Transition between the two hyperfine Levels of the Ground State of the Caesium-133 Atom.

Software.
Any set of Instructions for Hardware that makes it perform specific Operations. Computer Software consists of Programs, Libraries and related non-executable Data (such as online Documentation or digital Media).

SoundBlaster.
A Family of Sound Cards, released by Creative Technology Limited. Was the de-facto Standard for consumer Audio on the IBM PC compatible System Platform.

SVGA.
Super Video Graphics Array, Super VGA, a broad Term that covers a wide Range of Computer Display Standards.

US.
U.S., U.S.A., United States, United States of America. Country in west Part of Planet Earth. Territory first colonized by Spanish Empire in Year 1565 and Declared as Independant Country in Year 1776. Many Hardware and Software Corporations are located in the U.S.A.

Version.
A specific Form or Variation of Something. Etymology: from Latin "vertiõ" ("to turn"). Used to differentiate Software and Hardware released under the same Name. Hardware's Version is often called as Revision ("rev." shortly).

VESA.
Video Electronics Standards Association, is an international non-profit Corporation Standards Body for Computer Graphics formed in Year 1988.

VGA.
Video Graphics Array. An Amplitude Modulated Computer Display Standard, the 15-pin D-subminiature VGA Connector or the 640x480 Resolution.

Windows.
A series of closed-sourced Operating Systems by MS.

Windows 95.
An OS released by MS on August 24, 1995 (1995-08-24).

x86.
An Instructions Set of first 16-bit MicroProcessor by Intel.

y.
Year. Time/Event Measuring Unit. One (1) turn of a Planet around its Star. On Earth Planet, 1 y = ~365 d.
